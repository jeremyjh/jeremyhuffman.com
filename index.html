About my Work and Tech Interests

I’ve worked in technology for Bank of America since 1998. I presently manage Level 3 support in ATM Technology but have held many different positions over the years. I spent several years as a developer working mostly with .NET and related Microsoft technologies. I’ve been in various architecture and management roles (some with lots of hands-on, some not so much ) since 2006 but have continued to pursue technical interests on my own as well.

As a hobbyist I have done a few small projects in Ruby on Rails – my first started at around Rails version .10 but I have never done enough of it to really become proficient. The last year and the half I’ve been on a languages kick and have dabbled in several functional languages – starting with Odersky’s Functional Programming in Scala course at Coursera and then a few months dive into Erlang and Elixir. I wrote a couple of simple libraries in Elixir and recently found out some people are still using them! That is the neat part about contributing to very young and growing language communities I think – all the rough edges give you fun and useful opportunities. I have to admit that is a bit of a thrill.

Since last June I’ve been doing very little other than Haskell development. The learning curve is pretty steep but I think it has been worth it. I have one small library on Hackage – hopefully I will have meatier results worth sharing soon.

I’ve been hacking on Linux off and on since 1996 but in the last couple of years I’ve really gotten deeper into it, partly as a result of my job giving me the opportunity to play a pseudo-admin role on RHEL. At home and in my personal projects I use Ubuntu.
